Our Investment Philosophy

We believe that investing in high-quality businesses at reasonable prices is the most reliable path to long-term wealth creation. Our investment philosophy is rooted in the principles of value investing, combined with a deep understanding of business economics and competitive dynamics. We seek to identify companies with durable competitive advantages, strong management teams, and attractive valuations relative to their intrinsic value.

We approach investing with intellectual curiosity, humility, and patience. We think in terms of probabilities rather than certainties, and we are comfortable with ambiguity. Our decision-making process is deliberate and disciplined, free from the emotional biases that often lead to poor investment outcomes. We focus on what we can control: our process, our analysis, and our temperament.

We view every stock purchase as buying a piece of a real business. This ownership mindset fundamentally changes how we evaluate investments. Rather than speculating on short-term price movements, we focus on the underlying economics of the business: its competitive position, capital allocation track record, and long-term earnings power. This perspective naturally leads to a long holding period and low portfolio turnover.

We define great businesses as those with sustainable competitive advantages, high returns on invested capital, strong free cash flow generation, and management teams that allocate capital intelligently. These businesses tend to compound value over time, creating wealth for their owners. We seek to buy these businesses when market conditions provide us with a margin of safety, ensuring we pay a fair price for exceptional quality.

The Investment Framework

Industry stability

Focus on industries with predictable dynamics and stable competitive structures.

Business competitive advantages

Identify durable moats that protect long-term returns on capital.

Management incentives/allocation

Evaluate leadership alignment, capital allocation discipline, and shareholder orientation.

Qualitative long-shelf-life information

Prioritize enduring qualitative insights over short-term quantitative data.
